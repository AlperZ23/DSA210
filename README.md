Project Overview

In this project, the main goal is to analyse the relationship between food and energy prices, household income levels, and food consumption in the USA over the past 20 years. The project's  goal is to understand the fundamentals of how change in food prices relates to people buying trends. The dataset will combine public available food and energy prices, income level and consumption behaviors. Addinotliy, the dataset will be enriched with demographic and income-level information by region. This project focuses on analyzing socio economic factors that influence daily life behavior. Also it will include exploratory data analysis, statistical testing and machine learning models. 

### Objectives

1. **Understand Consumer Behavior**  
   Analyze how consumers alter their food and energy consumption patterns in response to changing food and energy prices.

2. **Predictive Modeling**  
    Models that estimate how price might change consumption decisions among different income levels.

3. **Data Visualization**  
    Use visuals to present insights and trends.

4. **Apply Data Science Techniques**  
    Implement techniques learned in class in a meaningful context.

Motivation

The rising cost of living and inflation are affecting everyday decisions, especially food and energy consumption trends. Understanding the relation between price changes and income is academically valuable and socially impactful. This project will:

1. Contribute understanding of economic resilience and inequality.  
2. Improve my data analysis skills using real-world, socially relevant data.  
3. Make an application in an occurrence that affects everyone—food access.

### Description of Dataset

The dataset will include:

1. **U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics:**  
   Prices of essential food and energy.  
2. **United States Census Bureau:**  
    Households income level.  
3. **Economic Research Service:**  
   Food Consumption & Demand  
   

Tools  and Technologies

1. **Python (Pandas, NumPy)**: Data preprocessing and manipulation.  
2. **Matplotlib**: Data visualization.  
3. **Statsmodels/SciPy**: Hypothesis testing and regression analysis.
